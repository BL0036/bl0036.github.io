About

For nearly eight years I've managed procurement for hospitals across Nepal — surgical and laboratory equipment, government tender documentation, vendor coordination, VAT and PAN paperwork. It's detailed, repetitive, deadline-driven work, and most of it was still being done by hand.

Rather than keep absorbing that manual load, I taught myself to build the software that removes it — starting with a full ERP built around my own daily workflow, then a quotation/document generator, a business ledger, and a challan/billing manager. I build using AI-assisted development, the way many developers now use Copilot or Cursor: I define the problem, design the data model, table formats, and business logic myself, then use AI to help implement it, and test everything against real daily use at my own office. Not everything I've built is in production yet — I'm upfront about which tools are live and which are still self-tested.

I'm not a computer science graduate, and I won't pretend to be a systems architect. What I bring instead is a working understanding of Nepali business operations, procurement, and compliance, paired with the ability to turn that understanding into software people actually rely on.

Side project (future venture, not current employment): I've also built the site for NovaCure SurgiMed, a surgical/hospital-supplies concept I'm developing independently — not yet an operating business.
